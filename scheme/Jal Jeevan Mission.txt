SCHEME NAME: Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)

DESCRIPTION:
A massive infrastructure program to provide a Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) to every rural household, school, Anganwadi center, Gram Panchayat building, and health center. The mission incorporates a community-based approach to water and includes mandatory source sustainability measures such as rainwater harvesting, water conservation, and greywater management. 

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
- Applicable to ALL households in rural India.
- Priority is given to quality-affected areas, villages in drought-prone and desert areas, and Sansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) villages.
- Applies to community buildings (Schools, Anganwadi centres, GP buildings, Health centres, wellness centres).

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:
- Any standard Proof of Identity is sufficient for the water connection (Voter ID, Passport, Driving License, Ration Card, or Aadhaar Card).

BENEFITS:
- Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC): Ensures safe drinking water supply directly to the household.
- Infrastructure Funding: The scheme funds exactly one tap connection per household. (Households are encouraged to have three delivery points: kitchen, washing/bathing, and toilet to maintain hygiene).
- Community Empowerment: Provides skill development and training for locals in plumbing, electrical work, water quality management, and water treatment.
- Sustainable Systems: Includes assistance for establishing sustainable water sources, infrastructure, and Operations & Maintenance (O&M) funds.

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. No Direct Application Required: The applicant does not need to submit an individual application online. 
2. State/UT Implementation: State Governments implement the mission based on a finalized State Action Plan (SAP).
3. Village Assessment: The District Water and Sanitation Mission (DWSM), in consultation with the Gram Panchayat, assesses the existing water supply infrastructure.
4. Infrastructure Rollout: FHTCs are provided via retrofitting old schemes, Single Village Schemes (SVS), Multi Village Schemes (MVS), or mini solar power-based piped water supply for isolated/tribal hamlets.
5. Provide ID: When the implementation reaches the village, the household simply provides a basic identity proof to register their connection.
6. Voluntary Ownership: The community is encouraged to contribute via cash, kind, or voluntary labor (shramdaan) to foster ownership.

ESTIMATED PROCESSING TIME: Subject to the State/UT rollout timeline and Gram Panchayat assessment schedule.

APPLICATION FEES: Free (Though community contribution/shramdaan is encouraged for operations and maintenance).

CONTACT INFORMATION:
Contact the local Gram Panchayat (GP), Village Water and Sanitation Committee (VWSC), or District Water and Sanitation Mission (DWSM).
